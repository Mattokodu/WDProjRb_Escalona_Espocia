Conway's Game of Life

The rules are simple. Each cell can be alive or dead. Each cell interacts with its eight neighbors (horizontal, vertical, diagonal) using the following rules: 1. Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbors dies (underpopulation). 2. Any live cell with two or three live neighbors lives on to the next generation. 3. Any live cell with more than three live neighbors dies (overpopulation). 4. Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbors becomes a live cell (reproduction).
