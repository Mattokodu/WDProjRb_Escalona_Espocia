Darwin's Theory of Evolution

Charles Darwin proposed natural selection as the main mechanism of evolution.

Key Ideas or Assumptions:

  1. Variation
    This is the idea that individuals in a population have differences in traits.
  2. Overproduction
    Organisms produce more offspring than the environment can support. So, eventually there will be a carrying capacity.
  3. Competition
    Because resources are limited, organisms compete to survive.
  4. Adaptation
    Some traits give certain individuals a better chance to survive.
  5. Natural Selection
    Individuals with advantageous traits survive and reproduce more, passing these traits on to future generations.
  6. Descent with Modification
    Over many generations, populations gradually change as helpful traits become more common.
