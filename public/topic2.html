We can use mathematics to model what happens to populations over time. An easy model is suppose we have \( x_n \) as the proportion of a species in the nth year out of the carrying capacity of its habitat. An easy simulation is that the population grows at a constant rate per year. So, \[ x_n = r x_{n-1}. \] However, this is not realistic as there is a carrying capacity. This is because while organisms grow exponentially, the resources only grow by a constant difference per year. This limits the population. A more accurate model is restricting \( x_n \), showing the proportion of the population compared to the carrying capacity. We can add a factor that \[ x_n = rx_{n-1}(1-x_{n-1}) \]
