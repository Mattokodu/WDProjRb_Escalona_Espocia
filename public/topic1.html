Unifying Themes of Biology

Properties of Life

Characteristics shared by living things. There are seven of them, which are the following:

  1. Order
    An organized structure
  2. Evolutionary adaptation
    Traits the improve; survival becomes more common.
  3. Regulation or Homeostasis
    The ability to maintain stable internal conditions
  4. Energy Processing
    Using energy for activities Homeostasis - the ability to maintain stable internal conditions
  5. Growth and Development
    Organisms growing and following genetic instructions
  6. Response to Environment
    Responding to stimuli
  7. Reproduction
    The production of offspring

Despite this, many still argue about whether certain things are considered living. For example, consider whether fire is living. You would most likely say it is not living, right? However, if we check it using the characteristics of life,

The Cell is the Basic Unit of Life

  • All living things are made out of cells
  • Cells carry out the basic functions of life
    • Ex. growth, energy use, reproduction

Structure and Function are Related

  • The way something is shaped helps determine what it can do
    • Ex. Bird wings are shaped for flight; Red blood cells are shaped to carry oxygen

Life Uses Energy

  • All organisms need energy to survive
  • Plants get energy from the sun; Animals get energy from food

Homeostasis

  • Living things keep stable internal conditions even when the environment changes
    • Ex. Shivering and sweating

Growth, Development, and Reproduction

  • Organisms grow and change over time.
  • They reproduce to create new life and continue on their species

Evolution

  • Populations change over long periods because of natural selection.
  • Evolution explains the diversity of life on Earth.

Unity and Diversity

  • All living things share common features (like DNA).
  • But they are also diverse due to evolution and adaptation.

Interdependence of Organisms

  • Living things depend on each other and their environment.
  • Example: plants produce oxygen that animals breathe.

Biological Systems

  • Life is organized into systems—molecules → cells → tissues → organs → organisms → ecosystems.
  • Each level works together to support life.
